# How to submit a government contract — start to finish

**Who this is for:** anyone. You do not need to know anything about government contracting. Jarvis does
the hard parts. You just tap a few buttons and do the one thing only a human is allowed to do: the final
"send it in."

There are two ways to do this. **The Wizard does all of this for you** — start there. The manual steps
below are the same thing, written out, in case you ever want to understand what's happening.

---

## The easy way: the Submit Wizard (recommended)

1. Open **Jarvis** on your phone or computer.
2. On the **Home** screen, look for the box that says **"YOUR NEXT MOVE."** Tap the blue button
   **"▸ Walk me through submitting this."**
   - (Or open the **Gov Pipeline** board and tap **"📋 Submit step-by-step"** on any opportunity.)
3. Now just follow the screens. There are six, one at a time:

   | Screen | What you do |
   |---|---|
   | **1. Should we go for it?** | Jarvis already checked if it's a good fit. Tap **"Yes — go for it"** (or "Skip it"). |
   | **2. Jarvis writes it** | Wait about a minute while Jarvis writes the whole proposal for you. |
   | **3. Read it over** | Skim it. You don't need to understand every line. If something's wrong, tap **"Change something"** and type what you want in plain words. |
   | **4. Safety check** | Jarvis checks it follows the government's rules. Green = good. If it finds a problem, tap **"Have Jarvis fix these."** |
   | **5. Send it in** | Jarvis tells you **exactly** where it goes — either copy the email it wrote, or follow the numbered steps on the government website. Then tap **"I sent it."** |
   | **6. Record it** | Paste the confirmation number if you got one (or just leave today's date). Tap **"Record it & finish."** |

4. Done. 🎉 You just submitted a government proposal. Jarvis tracks it from here and tells you when the
   agency responds.

**That's it.** If you can order food on an app, you can do this.

---

## What "send it in" means (screen 5, explained)

Government opportunities are submitted one of two ways. Jarvis figures out which one for you and shows you
the right instructions — you never have to guess:

- **By email** — Jarvis already wrote the whole email (who it goes to, the subject, the message). You tap
  **Copy**, open your email app, paste it, and hit Send. It comes from you, the owner, which is required.
- **On the SAM.gov website** — Jarvis can't log in as you, so you do this part. It gives you a button to
  open the exact page, a button to copy (or download) the proposal, and numbered steps: sign in → find the
  **"Respond"** button → paste/upload the proposal → submit. The site gives you a confirmation number.

> **Why doesn't Jarvis just send it automatically?** Because signing and submitting a federal proposal is
> a legal commitment that has to be made by a person — you. Jarvis does 95% of the work and makes your 5%
> take about a minute. (This is rule #2 of how Jarvis is built: it never sends, submits, or spends on its
> own — it always stops for your "yes.")

---

## If something goes wrong

- **"Jarvis couldn't write the proposal."** Tap back and try again — it's usually a momentary hiccup.
- **The safety check says FAIL.** Tap **"Have Jarvis fix these"** — it rewrites the proposal to fix the
  problems, then you re-read it.
- **You closed the wizard halfway.** No problem — reopen it from the same opportunity; nothing is lost, it
  picks up from your draft.
- **You submitted but forgot the confirmation number.** That's fine — just record the date. The proof is
  optional; what matters is the proposal went in.

---

## What happens after you submit

- The opportunity moves to the **"Submitted"** column on your Gov Pipeline board.
- Jarvis keeps watching for the agency's response and gives you a heads-up.
- If you **win**, mark it **Won** on the board — Jarvis then helps you line up the crew to do the work.

That's the whole journey: **find an opportunity → Jarvis writes the proposal → you read it → safety check →
send it in → done.** Repeat it a few times a week and you're running a government-contracting business.
